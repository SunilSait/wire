Our Standards

Our 7-Step Safety Protocol

Every technician follows these rigid safety checkpoints on every residential job site, ensuring a secure installation.

Step 1: Visual Inspection & Hazard Check

Prior to touching any wiring, our technician inspects the area for structural water hazards, old wiring deterioration, and signs of overloading.

Step 2: Circuit Voltage Verification Test

We use calibrated multimeters to verify that the active circuit and all secondary lines in the workspace are completely dead.

Step 3: Lockout / Tagout (LOTO) Implementation

The main panel breaker feeding the workspace is locked out and tagged to prevent accidental re-energizing by other home occupants.

Step 4: Insulation & Gauge check

We verify that wire gauge matches the breaker amperage limit and inspect cable insulation to ensure no split wiring exists.

Step 5: Ground & Continuity Testing

We verify solid metal-to-metal continuity and ground resistance to guarantee code-compliant safety grounding.

Step 6: Live Load Verification

Once energized, we perform load tests to ensure circuit voltage stability under nominal running conditions.

Step 7: Final Safety Sweep & Cleanup

We sweep the worksite for copper remnants, vacuum all debris from service panels, and complete a final safety code review.
